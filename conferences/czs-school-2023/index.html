About this Conference

The Conference in short

Interdisciplinary, highly interactive workshop on machine learning in the natural sciences with particular focus on astrophysics. The aim of this workshop is to bring together AI developers and domain experts to kick-start new and fascinating science projects.

General Structure

The workshop program consists of a well balanced mix of invited talks in the morning and plenty of free time in the afternoon for collaborative research, discussions and tutorials.

Target audience

Young researchers in the field of computer science, ML/AI, astrophysics or related

When

14.8.-18.8.2023

Where

Mathematikon Heidelberg

Program

Collaborative research projects for the afternoon will be defined in the weeks before the school starts from project suggestions made during registration.

We encourage applications that either propose projects for which tools, algorithms and architectures exists and data is needed or the other way round, project ideas and data exist but algorithms are needed.

Venue

The workshop will be held at the Interdisciplinary Center for Scientific Computing (IWR) in Heidelberg. More information about the IWR and its facilities and resources is available on the official Website.

IWR is accessible to disabled persons, and dedicated parking spaces are available. Unfortunately, the buses dedicated to the event and public transportations may not be accessible for everyone. Please contact us for specific information about your accessibility needs.
